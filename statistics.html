2. why do people do the things they do

For the longest time I could never understand why. Why did people do h ethings that they did. There were people around me that were super intense. Things had to be a certain way or it was wrong. I understood them on a surface level, but I never understood why. A simple common example of this this folding clothes. Why should we fold our clothes if we wear them later anyway. Why do anything for that matter.

One day I stopped doing it. Andt here were some key things I noticed.

  1. If I stopped these seemingly useless tasks, things because tangled and disordered. Making things more ordered took a longer time. So some of our action are to make the world a more ordered place.
  2. If I did the repetitive task for a number of repitions it becoame easier over time.

I discovered that why we do what we do is to make things more ordered. For example, cleaning. Even the purpose of all jobs is to make things more ordered. Teachers, you're ordering a classroom and teaching everyone one thing. The reason why we make companies is to provide one service to a lot of peoeple. That makes things more ordered. Even complex things like language tend to make things, concepts more ordered. But the question still stands, Why order things?

I think the answer is survival. Orderliness increases our chances of survival. An example of this is cleaning. Cleaning decreases the amount of bacteria in your house which effectively decreases the chances of a bacterial infection. This increses your chances of survival. We also build tools that order things. Something as simple as a broom, that helps sweep to big tech companies that help organize HR data.
